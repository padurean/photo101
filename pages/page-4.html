Side effects of exposure elements and focal length

"ISO" (sensor sensitivity)

Higher values allow more light to be captured, but they result in a grainier image (more noise).
Depending on your camera, other image quality aspects can be affected:
color vibrance/saturation, color depth/range, dynamic range, contrast.
Left half: low ISO value, Right half: high ISO value:

Shutter speed (exposure time)

Longer exposure times result in blurry photos (motion blur): either due to hand movement, either because elements in the frame are moving.

Aperture value

Larger apertures (smaller f-numbers) result in shorter depth of field (blurry foreground and background)





Smaller apertures (larger f-numbers) also result in sources of light having a star-like ("starburst") appearance (especially when using wide-angle lenses)

Focal length of the lens

Wide-angle lenses amplify the perspective effect (closer objects look bigger) and lengthen depth of field (less blurry background and foreground). Not good for portraits. Good for scenery, landscape.








Telephoto (longer) lenses diminish the perspective effect (compress planes - close and far objects seem more similar in size). Shorter depth of field (blurrier background and foreground). Good for portraits.

NOTE:
"Compression is a real thing but it really doesn't have anything to do with a lens. It has everything to do with your distance from the subject/background. You could get a shot with identical compression if you stand in the same location and take a photograph with a telephoto lens, a wide angle lens but crop in, or a camera with a "digital zoom." The magic isn't in the lens, it's in simply backing up."
